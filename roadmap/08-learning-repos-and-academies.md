# 📚 Learning repos & academies to mine

**The fastest way into GTM engineering is to clone what already works.** Two MIT-licensed repos, three free vendor academies, and two communities cover most of the on-ramp. Mine the repos for *both* structure and content (with license discipline), earn the free certs to speak the vendors' vocabulary, and lurk the communities to see the real questions hiring managers ask.

> [!TIP]
> License discipline matters if you plan to publish your own repo: **MIT / Apache / CC0 → adapt with attribution** (`License · Source · Authors`); **CC-BY-SA → link + commentary only, never copy verbatim.** All three repos below are MIT/CC0-friendly, so they're safe to fork and adapt with credit.

## Vendor academies (free certs)

| Academy | Link | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| **Clay University** | [university.clay.com](https://university.clay.com/) | Free courses + certs on waterfall enrichment, the credit economy, and Claygent AI agents. The category-definer's own curriculum. |
| **HubSpot Academy** | [academy.hubspot.com](https://academy.hubspot.com/) | Free Sales + Marketing + RevOps certs. The fastest way to speak CRM data-model and lifecycle-stage fluently. |
| **Make Academy** | [academy.make.com](https://academy.make.com/) | Free automation certs; visual scenario building and branching — the low-code iPaaS on-ramp. |

> [!NOTE]
> **Vendor certs are vocabulary, not proof of skill.** They teach you the tool's mental model and the words hiring managers use — worth the weekend. But a cert is not a portfolio; it's the [projects](07-portfolio-projects.md) that show you can build. Earn the cert to read the JD, then ship the repo to answer it.
